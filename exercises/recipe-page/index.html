DESSERT

CHOCOLATE ALMOND CROISSANT ROLLS

Think chocolate almond croissant, but ten times easier. Buttery, fluffy rolls filled with creamy almond goodness and melty chocolate, then topped with toasty almonds for that golden crunch. They taste like they came straight from a akery—except you made them in under an hour.

PREP TIME: 25 MINUTES COOK TIME: 15 MINUTES

TOTAL TIME: 40 MINUTES YIELDS: 12

INGREDIENTS

  • 1 pack (12 rolls) King’s Hawaiian sweet rolls
  • 1/2 cup milk or semi-sweet chocolate chips or chopped
    • chocolate chunks
  • 1/4 cup sliced almonds
  • powdered sugar, for dusting on top

ALMOND SIMPLE SYRUP

  • 1/4 cup water
  • 2 1/2 tablespoons granulated sugar
  • 1/2 teaspoon almond extract

ALMOND FILLING

  • 3/4 cup almond flour
  • 1/3 cup + 1 tablespoon granulated white sugar
  • 1/8 teaspoon salt
  • 6 tablespoons unsalted butter, room temperature
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1/4 teaspoon almond extract
  • 1 large egg

INSTRUCTIONS

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and line a baking dish or sheet tray with parchment paper.

    2. MAKE THE ALMOND SIMPLE SYRUP:

  2. In a small saucepan, combine water, sugar, and almond extract. Heat over medium heat, stirring until the sugar fully dissolves. Remove from heat and set aside to cool.

    3. MAKE THE ALMOND FILLING:

  3. In a mixing bowl, add almond flour, granulated sugar, a pinch of salt, unsalted butter, one egg, vanilla extract, and almond extract. Beat everything together until smooth and creamy. Reserve about ¼ cup of the filling to use for dolloping on top later.

    4. PREPARE AND BAKE THE ROLLS:

  4. Without pulling them apart, slice the entire pack of King’s Hawaiian rolls in half horizontally like a sandwich. Place the bottom half in your prepared dish.

  5. Brush the inside of both the bottom and top halves (cut sides only) with the almond simple syrup.

  6. Spread the majority of the almond filling evenly over the bottom half of the rolls. Sprinkle chocolate chips or chopped chocolate all over the filling.

  7. Place the top half of the rolls back on. Spoon little dollops of the reserved almond filling across the top of the rolls. Press sliced almonds into the filling.

  8. Bake uncovered for 12 to 15 minutes, or until the tops are golden and the filling on top is set and slightly crisped.

  9. Let cool slightly, dust with powdered sugar, and pull apart to serve warm and gooey!
